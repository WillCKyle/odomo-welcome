ODOMO

A lucrative app providing a dual power strategy for popular revolution.

The development strategy of Odomo has three stages:

1

Build an events-based platform with an innovative social feature to obtain revenue and users.

2

Introduce a public consensus-building system and incentivize event organizers to follow a mass line.

3

Develop a dual-power network with organization management tools and private consensus-building.

Entry into the market.

1.a

In the first stage, Odomo aims to replace ticketing vendors as the site that event attendees inevitably visit to RSVP or buy tickets.

Events are at the core of Odomo, which is focused on action and results. They are a key site of community building between small and medium businesses and the public.

You can receive half of the profits for a year from every organization you refer to the platform. Building the community is a cooperative venture.

Viral growth.

1.b

Odomo's key market differentiator at this stage is a new feature called Memories.

After an event ends, attendees receive a notification prompting them to share photos and videos from the event. In return, they get to see the uploads from other attendees.

After 48 hours, the event is automatically posted again as a Memory. The public gets to see what everyone experienced at the event.

For event organizers, a Memory is an opportunity to place advertisements. Itâ€™s in their interest to promote Odomo to make better Memories and gain a larger audience. This creates a revenue stream and a passive growth strategy.

Harnessing public discourse.

2

Next Odomo introduces Dialectical Consensus, a method for any number of users to reach a nuanced and actionable consensus. Dialectical Consensus is an innovation that lets people confront and reconcile the contradictions within public discourse.

Public Dialectical Consensus will govern Goals. Goals let organizers coordinate and publicize related events. A Goal contains a set of default and custom Questions. The consensus reached on these Questions has concrete effects, like renaming Goals or removing them.

Dialectical Consensus

Democratic de-centralism.

3

The third stage of development introduces private dialectical consensus within and between organizations.

Private consensus gives organizations a nuanced way to decide policy and or appoint personnel. Discussions among specialists can generate thought leadership to bring to public discussions.

We also introduce infrastructure for membership, roles and subdivisions. This asset will be offered as a paid service to businesses and other organizations.

Odomo may explore tools for users to form cooperative ventures and crowd fund using smart contracts.
